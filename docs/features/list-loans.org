#+title: List Loans

* Feature: List Loans
** Background:
*** Given a lender with an active loan: borrower "Aaron", amount $10,000, 12% annual rate, 12 months, started 2026-05-01
*** Payment due dates are derived monthly from the loan start date (first due date is one month after start).
*** A period is considered covered when payments within that period sum to at least the scheduled monthly payment amount.
*** A loan is overdue when its next due date is strictly in the past and the period is not fully covered.
** Scenario: Lender views the loan list with active loans
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then the page shows the "Active Loans" section
*** And each loan shows: borrower name, amount borrowed, start date, and next payment due date
*** And loans are sorted newest first (by start date, most recent at top)
** Scenario: Loan shows next payment due date
*** Given the loan started on 2026-05-01 and no payments have been recorded
*** When the lender views the loans page on 2026-05-15
*** Then the loan shows next payment date as 2026-06-01
** Scenario: Next payment date advances after a period is fully covered
*** Given the loan started on 2026-05-01 and the lender recorded a payment of $933.33 on 2026-06-01
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then the loan shows next payment date as 2026-07-01
** Scenario: Overdue loan is flagged
*** Given the loan started on 2026-05-01 and no payments have been recorded
*** When the lender views the loans page on 2026-06-02
*** Then the loan shows "Overdue (2026-06-01)" instead of the next payment date
** Scenario: Partial payment does not clear the overdue flag
*** Given the loan has a scheduled payment of $933.33 due on 2026-06-01
*** And the lender recorded a payment of $200 on 2026-06-01
*** When the lender views the loans page on 2026-06-02
*** Then the loan still shows "Overdue (2026-06-01)"
** Scenario: Loan on its due date is not yet overdue
*** Given the loan has a payment due on 2026-06-01 and no payments recorded
*** When the lender views the loans page on 2026-06-01
*** Then the loan shows next payment date as 2026-06-01 (not flagged as overdue)
** Scenario: Next payment reflects lower interest after an extra principal payment
*** Given the loan has had one regular payment and one extra payment of $2,000
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then the next payment due date advances based on covered periods, not on the extra payment amount
** Scenario: Paid-off loans appear in a separate section
*** Given the lender has one active loan and one fully paid-off loan
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then the page shows two sections: "Active Loans" and "Paid Off"
*** And the paid-off loan appears only in the "Paid Off" section
*** And the paid-off loan shows: borrower name, amount borrowed, and start date
*** And the paid-off loan does not show a next payment date
** Scenario: Distinguishing loans from the same borrower
*** Given the lender has two loans for borrower "Aaron": one for $10,000 started 2026-01-15 and one for $10,000 started 2026-05-01
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then both loans are listed with their respective start dates, allowing the lender to tell them apart
** Scenario: Empty state when lender has no loans
*** Given the lender has no loans
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then the page shows a message indicating there are no loans yet
*** And the page shows a link to create a new loan
** Scenario: Cross-account isolation
*** Given another lender has a different loan
*** When the lender views the loans page
*** Then only the lender's own loans are listed
