#+title: Record A Payment

* Feature: Record a Payment
** Background:
*** Given a lender with an active loan: borrower "aaron", amount $10,000, 12% annual rate, 12 months
*** Interest accrues monthly from the loan start date. Payments apply to interest first, then principal. Once a period's interest is covered, further payments within that period apply entirely to principal.
*** The lender provides the payment date (e.g., to record a payment received yesterday). Defaults to today if omitted. Future dates are not allowed.
** Scenario: Lender records a scheduled monthly payment
*** When the lender records a payment of $933.33 for the loan
*** Then the lender is redirected to the Loan detail page
*** And the remaining balance shows $9,166.67
*** And the payment appears in the payment history showing: amount, date, principal applied, and interest applied
** Scenario: Lender records an extra principal payment
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $9,166.67 (one regular payment already made)
*** When the lender records a payment of $2,000
*** Then the remaining balance shows $7,166.67
*** And the payment history shows $2,000 applied entirely to principal with $0 interest
** Scenario: Lender records a partial payment (less than scheduled amount)
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $10,000 and interest due is $100
*** When the lender records a payment of $200
*** Then the remaining balance shows $9,900
*** And the payment shows: $100 interest applied, $100 principal applied
** Scenario: Lender records a payment that only covers interest
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $10,000 and interest due is $100
*** When the lender records a payment of $50
*** Then the remaining balance shows $10,000
*** And the payment shows: $50 interest applied, $0 principal applied
** Scenario: Scheduled payment after an extra principal payment reflects lower interest
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $7,166.67 (one regular payment and one extra payment of $2,000)
*** When the lender records a payment of $905.00
*** Then the lender is redirected to the Loan detail page
*** And the remaining balance shows $6,333.34
*** And the payment shows: $71.67 interest applied, $833.33 principal applied
** Scenario: Lender views payment history for a loan
*** Given the loan has 3 recorded payments
*** When the lender views the loan detail page
*** Then the payment history lists all 3 payments in reverse chronological order
*** And each payment shows: date, amount, principal portion, and interest portion
** Scenario: Lender records a payment that fully pays off the loan
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $933.33 and interest due is $100
*** When the lender records a payment of $1,033.33
*** Then the remaining balance shows $0.00
*** And the loan detail page shows a "paid off" indicator
*** And the loan list page shows a "paid off" indicator next to the loan
** Scenario: Payment requires a positive amount
*** When the lender submits a payment with no amount
*** Then the form shows a validation error
** Scenario: Payment date cannot be in the future
*** When the lender submits a payment with a date in the future
*** Then the form shows a validation error
** Scenario: Payment cannot exceed remaining balance
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $5,000
*** When the lender tries to record a payment of $6,000
*** Then the form shows a validation error that the amount exceeds the balance
** Scenario: Cross-account isolation
*** Given another lender has a different loan
*** When the lender tries to record a payment on the other lender's loan
*** Then the payment is not found
