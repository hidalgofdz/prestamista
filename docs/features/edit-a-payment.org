#+title: Edit A Payment

* Feature: Edit a Payment
** Background:
*** Given a lender with an active loan: borrower "aaron", amount $10,000, 12% annual rate, 12 months
*** Interest accrues monthly from the loan start date. Payments apply to interest first, then principal.
*** The loan has a recorded payment of $933.33 (the first scheduled payment): $100 interest applied, $833.33 principal applied. Remaining balance: $9,166.67.
** Scenario: Lender edits a payment amount
*** When the lender edits the payment amount from $933.33 to $1,200
*** Then the lender is redirected to the Loan detail page
*** And the payment shows: $100 interest applied, $1,100 principal applied
*** And the remaining balance shows $8,900
** Scenario: Lender edits a payment date
*** When the lender edits the payment date to a different valid date within the same period
*** Then the payment is updated successfully
*** And the interest/principal split is recalculated for the new date
** Scenario: Lender edits a payment to a lower amount
*** When the lender edits the payment amount from $933.33 to $500
*** Then the payment shows: $100 interest applied, $400 principal applied
*** And the remaining balance shows $9,600
** Scenario: Editing a payment recalculates subsequent payments
*** Given the loan has two recorded payments:
**** Payment 1: $933.33 on month 1 ($100 interest, $833.33 principal). Balance: $9,166.67.
**** Payment 2: $933.33 on month 2 ($91.67 interest, $841.66 principal). Balance: $8,325.01.
*** When the lender edits Payment 1 amount from $933.33 to $500
*** Then Payment 1 shows: $100 interest applied, $400 principal applied
*** And the remaining balance after Payment 1 is $9,600
*** And Payment 2 is recalculated: $96.00 interest applied, $837.33 principal applied
*** And the remaining balance shows $8,762.67
** Scenario: Date edit reorders payments on the timeline
*** Given the loan has two recorded payments:
**** Payment 1: $500 on Jan 15
**** Payment 2: $933.33 on Feb 15
*** When the lender edits Payment 1 date from Jan 15 to Mar 15
*** Then Payment 2 is now chronologically first and recalculated accordingly
*** And Payment 1 (now chronologically second) is recalculated against the updated balance
** Scenario: Edit is rejected when downstream payment becomes invalid
*** Given the loan has two recorded payments:
**** Payment 1: $933.33 on month 1
**** Payment 2: $9,000 on month 2
*** When the lender edits Payment 1 amount from $933.33 to $200
*** Then the form shows a validation error that the change would make a subsequent payment exceed the remaining balance
*** And no payments are modified
** Scenario: Lender replaces proof of payment
*** Given the payment has an existing proof image attached
*** When the lender edits the payment and uploads a new proof image
*** Then the old proof is replaced by the new one
*** And the payment history shows the new proof thumbnail
** Scenario: Editing without uploading new proof preserves existing proof
*** Given the payment has an existing proof image attached
*** When the lender edits only the payment amount (without uploading new proof)
*** Then the existing proof remains attached
** Scenario: Lender deletes proof of payment
*** Given the payment has an existing proof image attached
*** When the lender deletes the proof from the payment
*** Then the proof is removed
*** And no thumbnail is shown for that payment in the history
** Scenario: Deleting proof does not affect payment details
*** Given the payment has proof attached, amount $933.33, and date Jan 15
*** When the lender deletes the proof
*** Then the payment still shows amount $933.33 and date Jan 15
** Scenario: Editing a payment on a paid-off loan can reactivate it
*** Given the loan is paid off (remaining balance is $0)
*** When the lender edits the last payment to a lower amount
*** Then the remaining balance is greater than $0
*** And the loan no longer shows the "paid off" indicator
** Scenario: Edit requires a positive amount
*** When the lender edits a payment and sets the amount to 0
*** Then the form shows a validation error
** Scenario: Edit date cannot be in the future
*** When the lender edits a payment and sets the date to a future date
*** Then the form shows a validation error
** Scenario: Edit amount cannot exceed remaining balance
*** Given the loan has a remaining balance of $5,000 (excluding this payment's principal)
*** When the lender edits the payment amount to $6,000
*** Then the form shows a validation error that the amount exceeds the balance
** Scenario: Proof upload on edit must be a valid file type
*** When the lender edits a payment and uploads a .txt file as proof
*** Then the form shows a validation error that only images and PDFs are accepted
** Scenario: Proof upload on edit must not exceed 10 MB
*** When the lender edits a payment and uploads a 15 MB image as proof
*** Then the form shows a validation error that the file is too large
** Scenario: Cross-account isolation
*** Given another lender has a different loan with a payment
*** When the lender tries to edit the other lender's payment
*** Then the payment is not found
