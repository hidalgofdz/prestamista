#+title: Sign Up and Login

* Feature: Sign Up and Login
** Notes:
*** Auth method: magic links only (passwordless, no passkeys for now)
*** Magic link expiry: 15 minutes
*** Magic link URL: ~/sessions/verify?token=abc123~
*** Email service: Resend (letter_opener in development, ActionMailer test mode in tests)
*** Sign up creates both a User and an Account (account name defaults to user name)
*** Token implementation: Rails ~generates_token_for~ with 15-minute expiry
*** Login request always shows same message regardless of whether email exists (prevents enumeration)
*** Session: server-side model (revocable), referenced by encrypted cookie
*** Account derived from session (not URL) — routes are ~/loans~, ~/borrowers~ (no ~/:account_id~ prefix)
*** Root path: ~/loans~ (authenticated), login page (unauthenticated)
*** Refactoring existing URL-based multi-tenancy is part of this feature
** Background:
*** Given the app has no existing users
** Scenario: Lender signs up
*** When a new lender signs up with email "hidalgo@example.com" and name "Hidalgo"
*** Then the lender is redirected to the Loans page
*** And an account is created for the lender
** Scenario: Lender logs in via magic link
*** Given a lender with email "hidalgo@example.com" already exists
*** When the lender requests a magic link to "hidalgo@example.com"
*** Then an email is sent with a login link
*** When the lender clicks the login link within 15 minutes
*** Then the lender is redirected to the Loans page
** Scenario: Magic link expires after 15 minutes
*** Given a lender with email "hidalgo@example.com" already exists
*** When the lender clicks a magic link that is older than 15 minutes
*** Then the lender sees an error that the link has expired
*** And the lender is shown the login page to request a new link
** Scenario: Sign up with existing email
*** Given a lender with email "hidalgo@example.com" already exists
*** When a new lender tries to sign up with email "hidalgo@example.com"
*** Then the form shows a validation error that the email is already taken
** Scenario: Lender logs out
*** Given a lender is logged in
*** When the lender clicks log out
*** Then the lender is redirected to the login page
*** And the lender can no longer access the Loans page
** Scenario: Unauthenticated lender is redirected to login
*** When an unauthenticated user tries to access the Loans page
*** Then the user is redirected to the login page
